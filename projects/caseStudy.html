Developing the Strategy

I sat down with Kendall Keeter, the business owner, to craft the brand's strategy and new identity. Over a facilitated session, I unsurfaced the challenges the business faced. In the process, we were able to prioritized the needs and goals of the business and its customers. This became the foundation for the branding and marketing plans of her website and social media platforms.

Understanding the Users

To understand the needs of her customers, I created unique profiles to represent the range of clients. This painted a clear picture of their demographics, psychographics, needs, and wants.

Positioning

Understanding what the KKP brand is and who Kendall is a champion for, helped define the positioning statment of the organization:

Experience your fairytale; full of dreams, magic, and adventure.

Phase Two

Brand Refresh

I developed an entire identity system to make sure every client's touch-point reflected the brand. The rebrand included updating the logo, introducing a new color palette, a new typeface, and collateral pieces. This created a more cohesive experience for both the photoshoot and its digital extensions.

Logo

I decided to create a hand drawn logo for Kendall to create a personalized feeling. I created a monogram combining the letter K and an infinity sign.

Finding the Right Type

I updated the typeface of the brand to be modern and clean, and another to leave a personal touch. Both typefaces are flexible enough to work wellacross large printed displays down to small digital screens.

Brand Colors

The objective was to create a stress free experience for mothers and children during kendall's photography sessions. I wanted the colors to reflect that by being calm and dreamy.

Phase Three

User Experience

While the overarching business goal was to make it easier for client's to book a consultation, there were several user goals that needed to be addressed and accomplished. I was able to strike a nice balance between bookings consultations and helping clinets plan for their photoshoot experience.

Mobile Design

A significant percentage fo Kendall's clients view on mobil devices. One of my primary goals was to craft the userinterface design to be modular, in order to achieve perfect responsiveness, no matter the screen size.
